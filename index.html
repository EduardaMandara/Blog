Did you know about it?

  1. Coffee is the second most popular beverage in the world, behind only water;
  2. The origin of coffee is Ethiopia. It was called "buna" by the first coffee drinkers. These people lived on the island of Kaffa and used the ingredient in a ritual in which they prepared pure coffee. In this sense, some Muslim communities used the whole coffee (branch, foliage, beans). Even today, it is possible to find people in the villages led by the scents of "buna";
  3. Europeans got to know coffee through the Arabs, since the great commercialization pole for the beans was the port of Yemen. That is why, among the Europeans, the first name used to define the drink was "Arab wine";
  4. Coffea arabica is the scientific name for the Arabica coffee species. The beans have less caffeine than Coffea canephora, or Robusta (or Conilon);
  5. Arabica coffee has natural sweetness and diverse sensory experiences, whereas, Robusta beans are more bitter, lack sensory aspects, and have low quality;
  6. The French Mission symbolizes the French mission in the coffee universe. In short, it was the French missionaries who took coffee to the world;
  7. There is a strong belief in many cultures that shows the future from coffee grounds. The technique is known as caffeomancy.
